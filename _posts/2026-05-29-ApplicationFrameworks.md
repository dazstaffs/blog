I have recently been reading a really good book by a former Meta Engineer, Austen McDonald, called "Mastering Behavioural Interviews". The relevance is I have been struggling with interviews a lot recently. On paper, my CV is detailed yet accurate and when I originally departed my last job, I thought I would easily find a new job, but in interviews I over-talk myself out of the door. I have had interviews with Allianz, AXA, AXA Health, Admiral insurance, Tokio Marine, Dale Underwriting, Zurich insurance, Just Group, Clarion Events... I could go on but you get my point.

In this post I'm going to give you 5 tips for improving your interviewing skills as a manager from the book I have just mentioned.

1. Frameworks - when you answer a hypothetical question, you should pick a framework before answering and there are many frameworks to choose from. If you want to reduce the number of support tickets coming into your queue, take a look at EAD (Eliminate, Automate, Delegate). If you are or we a product manager being asked about Product Lifecycles, take a look at the PLC phases (Introduction, Growth, Maturity & Decline) and Gartner's TIME framework(Tolerate, Invest, Migrate, Eliminate). If you work in DevOps or software, think about DORA(DevOp Research & Assessment). Microsoft also have some frameworks such as the 6Rs for the migration & eliminating of applications (Rehost, Replatform, Refactor, Rebuild, Replace, Retire).
